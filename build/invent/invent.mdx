Invent is Relevance AI's creation flow: you describe what you want in plain language and Inventor generates a working agent, tool, or workforce for you. You build directly in Relevance AI — there's no external AI client or MCP setup to wire up first.

Inventor doesn't only create from scratch. Point it at an existing agent, tool, or workforce and describe the change you want, and it edits what you already have.

If you've used a coding or AI-client workflow (such as Claude Code) to assemble agents and tools, Invent covers the same ground inside the platform. You describe the outcome, Inventor builds the first version, and you iterate from there.

## How to use Invent

<Warning>
  Invent is expensive to run and consumes significant credits. Use it deliberately and keep an eye on your usage.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Inventor panel">
    Open Inventor from the side panel on a tool, agent, or workforce page, or go to the `/invent/` page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe what you want">
    Write what you want to build, edit, or analyze in plain language. Be specific about the outcome you're after.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and iterate">
    Inventor generates a first version or applies your edit. Review it, then refine by describing the changes you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect integrations">
    Connect any integrations the agent, tool, or workforce needs to run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inventor settings

Inventor settings tailor how Invent works for you and your project. They apply to Invent v3 (the Inventor panel and the `/invent/` page), not the legacy Blackbox tool Invent flow at `/tool/.../invent`.

### About You

About You is a free-text field of up to 5,000 characters, set per project. Set it once and it persists for that project. Inventor injects it into the model's user context to tailor tone, domain assumptions, and personalization.

It's descriptive only — it shapes how Inventor talks to you and what it assumes about your work, but it doesn't change Inventor's role or hardcode any tool configuration.

Describe who you are and what you're working on. For example:

- A recruiter sourcing candidates and screening applications.
- A developer building internal tooling and integrations.
- A marketer running campaigns and analyzing performance.

### Save and reference memories

Turn on this toggle to let Inventor remember specific facts across sessions — for example, "always return JSON, not markdown." This is separate from the static About You text: use About You for who you are, and memories for standing instructions you want Inventor to carry between sessions.

### Model

The model dropdown sets which LLM powers your Invent session. Choose from the configurable list; there's a default if you don't change it.
