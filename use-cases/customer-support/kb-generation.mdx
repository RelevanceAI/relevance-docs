Every support team has the same problem: the KB is the most leveraged thing in the operation, and it's the thing nobody has time to write. A KB Agent watches resolved tickets, spots the patterns worth documenting, and drafts the articles — so the next ticket of that shape gets a faster answer.

## The shape of this motion

A KB Agent takes a resolved ticket (or cluster of tickets) and returns a publishable article.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Inputs" icon="arrow-right-to-bracket">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Sources" icon="globe">
    Ticket conversations, agent resolution notes, product changelog, your KB style and structure guides.

## Climb the ladder

### L1 — Ask the Agent in chat

Clone a support Agent from the [Marketplace](/get-started/marketplace/introduction) — or [build one](/build/introduction) — and open it in chat. A KB owner asks the Agent to surface gaps or draft an article from a specific ticket cluster.

Example prompts:

- *"Look at the last 50 resolved tickets in the 'billing' category — what 3 articles should we write that we don't have?"*
- *"Turn this ticket into a KB article. Customer hit the SSO bug last week and we resolved with workaround X."*
- *"What's the most common search query on our help site this month that has no matching article?"*

### L2 — Give it a playbook

When the same article shape repeats, codify it:

- Define article structure, tone, and target length in the [prompt](/build/agents/build-your-agent/prompt)
- Upload your KB style guide, existing top-performing articles, and category taxonomy to [Knowledge](/build/knowledge/create-knowledge)
- Use [Bulk Schedule](/build/agents/give-your-agent-tasks/bulk-schedule) to draft articles from the last quarter of resolved tickets in one pass

### L3 — Run it on pipeline signals

Wrap the Agent in a workflow:

- A ticket resolves with high CSAT → if no matching KB article exists, the Agent drafts one and posts to the KB owner
- Same issue resolved 5+ times in a quarter → Agent drafts a consolidated article and tags it for review
- A new product feature ships → Agent reviews tickets mentioning the feature and proactively drafts FAQs

See [Triggers](/build/agents/build-your-agent/triggers) and [Workforces](/build/workforces/create-a-workforce).

### L4 — Let it tune itself

The Agent learns which articles actually got read, searched, and deflected tickets. Drops formats that don't help self-serve users; doubles down on the shapes that do — your KB evolves toward what your customers actually need.

## Common pitfalls

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Articles that paraphrase one ticket" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Drafting from a single ticket gives you an article that only solves that exact case. Cluster similar tickets first so the article generalizes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Stale or contradictory content" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Auto-drafting without checking existing articles produces overlap and contradiction. Have the Agent search the KB first and propose an *edit* if an article already covers it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Auto-publishing without review" icon="circle-exclamation">
    KB articles are externally visible — bad ones cost trust. Always gate publishing with a human editor until you've watched it for two quarters.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No tie to actual search demand" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Drafting articles agents *would have used* is fine, but missing the bigger win: articles for what customers *search and can't find*. Connect the Agent to KB search analytics.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
