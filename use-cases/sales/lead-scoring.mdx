Not every lead deserves the same effort. A scoring Agent evaluates incoming leads against your ICP and buying signals so reps focus on the accounts most likely to close — and disqualify the ones that won't.

## When this pays off

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="High inbound volume" icon="inbox">
    Leads are arriving faster than reps can manually triage them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Equal time on tire-kickers" icon="scale-unbalanced">
    Reps are spending the same hour on a Fortune 500 prospect as a free-trial signup with no fit.
  </Card>
  <Card title="MQLs losing momentum" icon="hourglass-half">
    Marketing-qualified leads are sitting unrouted while marketing complains the funnel is broken.
  </Card>
  <Card title="ICP exists on paper only" icon="file">
    You have an ICP doc but the team isn't applying it consistently across new leads.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The shape of this motion

A scoring Agent takes a lead record and returns a qualification assessment.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Inputs" icon="arrow-right-to-bracket">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Sources" icon="globe">

## Climb the ladder

### L1 — Ask the Agent in chat

[Build a scoring Agent](/build/introduction) and open it in chat. A rep or AE drops a lead in and asks for a quick read before committing time.

Example prompts:

- *"Score this lead — they came in from the pricing page yesterday. Is it worth a first call?"*
- *"Why did we lose Globex last quarter, and is this new account similar?"*
- *"Walk me through the top 10 new leads from this week and what to do with each."*

See the [Marketplace](/get-started/marketplace/introduction) for related Agents to adapt.

### L2 — Give it a playbook

Codify your qualification framework as a reusable Agent:

- Upload your ICP, persona definitions, and disqualification rules to [Knowledge](/build/knowledge/create-knowledge)
- Build the scoring Agent with a [prompt](/build/agents/build-your-agent/prompt) that produces consistent fit and intent scores plus reasoning
- Run it on a backfill of existing leads via [Bulk Schedule](/build/agents/give-your-agent-tasks/bulk-schedule) to validate the scoring matches your team's intuition

### L3 — Run it on pipeline signals

Wrap the Agent in a workflow:

- New lead lands in the CRM → scored within seconds, written back to the record
- Score crosses a threshold → routed to the right rep and posted to Slack
- Lead is enriched or updated → re-evaluated against the new data

See [Triggers](/build/agents/build-your-agent/triggers) and [Workforces](/build/workforces/create-a-workforce).

### L4 — Let it tune itself

The scoring model retrains on win/loss outcomes — your closed-won deals teach it what "qualified" actually means. ICP definitions shift based on what closed, not what someone wrote a year ago.

## Common pitfalls

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scoring without enrichment first" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Thin CRM data produces unreliable scores. Enrich the record before you ask the Agent to judge it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Treating the score as truth" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Reps stop using their own judgment if the number is treated as final. The score is an input — surface the reasoning so reps can override it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Stale ICP definitions" icon="circle-exclamation">
    If the ICP in Knowledge hasn't been touched in 18 months, the Agent is judging against last year's reality. Review it quarterly against actual close-won data.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No feedback loop from outcomes" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Without won/lost deals flowing back into the scoring criteria, the model can't learn. Pipe deal outcomes back to the Agent's evals.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
