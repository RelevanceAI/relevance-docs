Reps shouldn't be scrambling for context five minutes before a call. A meeting brief Agent pulls together everything relevant about the account — recent conversations, deal history, company updates — into a snapshot a rep can scan in under a minute.

## When this pays off

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Back-to-back calendars" icon="calendar-days">
    Reps run meetings in sequence with no buffer to prep between them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Context scattered everywhere" icon="puzzle-piece">
    Account history lives across CRM, Slack, support tickets, and email — and key details get missed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="New reps inheriting accounts" icon="user-plus">
    Reps joining mid-deal need to come up to speed without reading every email thread.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Exec drop-ins on deals" icon="user-tie">
    Leadership joins late-stage calls and needs the picture in two minutes, not twenty.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The shape of this motion

A meeting brief Agent compiles a one-page snapshot of the account ahead of a scheduled call.

  <Card title="Inputs" icon="arrow-right-to-bracket">

  <Card title="Sources" icon="globe">

## Climb the ladder

### L1 — Ask the Agent in chat

[Build a brief Agent](/build/introduction) and open it in chat. A rep asks right before a call.

Example prompts:

- *"What do I need to know about Acme Corp before my 3pm? Pull the last three emails and recent deal activity."*
- *"Summarize where we are with Globex — last call notes, open objections, next steps."*
- *"Who from Initech is on my calendar tomorrow and what should I prep on each of them?"*

See the [Marketplace](/get-started/marketplace/introduction) for related pre-built Agents you can adapt.

### L2 — Give it a playbook

Build a reusable brief Agent:

- Define your preferred brief format in the [prompt](/build/agents/build-your-agent/prompt) — length, sections, tone
- Upload account templates, your sales methodology, and key questions per deal stage into [Knowledge](/build/knowledge/create-knowledge)
- Connect CRM, email, and calendar so the brief draws on the full account picture

### L3 — Run it on pipeline signals

Wrap the Agent in a workflow:

- A calendar event is scheduled → brief generated 24 hours before, emailed to the rep
- A deal moves to a key stage (e.g. proposal) → exec-style brief generated for leadership review
- A support ticket opens on an account with an active deal → brief regenerated and sent to the AE

See [Triggers](/build/agents/build-your-agent/triggers), [Google Calendar](/integrations/popular-integrations/google-calendar), and [Workforces](/build/workforces/create-a-workforce).

### L4 — Let it tune itself

The brief format adapts to what reps actually use vs skip — which sections they cite in calls, which they ignore. Per-rep style tuning emerges. Briefs get shorter where shorter works, deeper where deeper matters.

## Common pitfalls

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Briefs that are too long" icon="circle-exclamation">
    If the brief takes more than a minute to scan, reps stop reading. Cap section lengths in the prompt and force prioritization.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Static account data with no recency" icon="circle-exclamation">
    A brief that just regurgitates the CRM record isn't useful. The Agent has to surface what's *new* — last contact, last news, last ticket.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Same depth for every meeting" icon="circle-exclamation">
    A discovery call and a renewal call need different briefs. Branch the prompt by meeting type or deal stage.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Raw data instead of insight" icon="circle-exclamation">
    "2 open tickets" is data. "Two open critical tickets opened this week — both about billing" is a talking point. Have the Agent interpret, not just enumerate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
