Generic outreach gets ignored. Hand-crafted messages get replies — but they don't scale to a list of 500 prospects. An outbound Agent uses real prospect context to draft personalized messages at the volume your pipeline needs.

## When this pays off

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="High outbound volume" icon="paper-plane">
    SDRs running 100+ touches a week and personalization is collapsing into copy-paste.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flatlining reply rates" icon="chart-line-down">
    The team is writing more emails and getting fewer replies — the messages stopped sounding different.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Top-10% personalization" icon="medal">
    Reps only hand-personalize their A-list. Everyone else gets a template they're not happy with.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-channel sequences" icon="shuffle">
    Email + LinkedIn + follow-up timing is breaking down because no human is orchestrating it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

An outbound Agent takes a prospect and produces a message ready to send.

## Climb the ladder

### L1 — Let SuperGTM do it

A rep pastes a prospect into chat and asks for a message. SuperGTM uses connected CRM, LinkedIn, and web tools to draft on demand.

Example prompts:

- *"Draft a LinkedIn DM to Sarah at Initech — she just got promoted to VP of Sales last month."*
- *"Write a follow-up email to Acme Corp referencing our last call notes in HubSpot."*
- *"Give me three subject line options for a re-engagement email to dormant trial users."*

See [SuperGTM](/get-started/chat/super-gtm) for setup.

### L2 — Give it a playbook

Lift the patterns out of chat into a reusable Agent. Two paths:

- **Clone a template** — start from [LinkedIn Outreach](https://marketplace.relevanceai.com/listing/5508c7f6-f3a7-4593-8faf-22fbecc4e0d4) and adapt the prompt to your tone and value props
- **Configure SuperGTM** — upload messaging playbooks, value props, and tone-of-voice guides to Knowledge so every draft sounds like you

See the [Marketplace](/get-started/marketplace/introduction) and [Knowledge](/build/knowledge/create-knowledge).

### L3 — Run it on pipeline signals

Wrap the Agent in a workflow that fires automatically. Common triggers:

- A new lead enters HubSpot → first-touch email drafted and queued for rep approval
- A LinkedIn connection is accepted → follow-up DM drafted automatically
- A prospect engages with content → re-engagement sequence drafts itself

See [Triggers](/build/agents/build-your-agent/triggers) and [Bulk Schedule](/build/agents/give-your-agent-tasks/bulk-schedule).

### L4 — Let it tune itself

The Agent A/B tests subject lines, opening hooks, CTAs, and follow-up timing against reply rates and meetings booked. It evolves its own playbook based on what actually converts for your team, not your last guess.

## Common pitfalls

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Personalization that's surface-level" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Mentioning the company name doesn't count. The opening line has to reference something the prospect would only believe you noticed if you actually paid attention — a post, a launch, a role change.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sending too fast" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Volume plus zero warm-up equals the spam folder. Use sending caps and a warm-up schedule on new domains.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Every message reads the same" icon="circle-exclamation">
    If your reply rates flatline as volume grows, the Agent has converged on a template. Force structural variation in the prompt — different opens, different CTA placements.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No human gate on exec accounts" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Use [Alerts](/build/agents/build-your-agent/alerts) to route messages for high-value accounts through human review before they send.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
