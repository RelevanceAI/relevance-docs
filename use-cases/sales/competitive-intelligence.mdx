Deals get lost when reps can't articulate how you compare. A competitive intelligence Agent monitors what competitors are doing and keeps your team's battle cards current — so reps walk into positioning conversations ready, not improvising.

## When this pays off

  <Card title="Competitors shipping fast" icon="rocket">
    The market is moving faster than your battle cards can keep up.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Out-of-date positioning" icon="calendar-xmark">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Losing winnable deals" icon="chart-line-down">
    Win/loss reviews keep flagging the same competitor as the reason — and the same gaps.

## The shape of this motion

A competitive intelligence Agent monitors a defined competitor set and surfaces what matters.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Inputs" icon="arrow-right-to-bracket">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Sources" icon="globe">

## Climb the ladder

### L1 — Let SuperGTM do it

A rep asks during a deal where a competitor surfaces.

Example prompts:

- *"What did Acme Corp announce last quarter? They came up on a call this morning."*
- *"Pull a quick comparison of our pricing vs. Globex for an exec on this deal."*
- *"Summarize where we stand against Initech for a renewal conversation tomorrow."*

See [SuperGTM](/get-started/chat/super-gtm) for setup.

### L2 — Give it a playbook

Codify the patterns:

- Build an Agent that tracks your top 5–10 competitors with a [prompt](/build/agents/build-your-agent/prompt) defining what counts as a signal
- Upload existing battle cards, positioning docs, and win/loss notes into [Knowledge](/build/knowledge/create-knowledge) so the Agent builds on what you already have
- Add web search and [Firecrawl](/build/tools/tool-steps/firecrawl) for crawling competitor sites and news

### L3 — Run it on pipeline signals

Wrap the Agent in a workflow:

- Weekly scan → digest posted to Slack every Monday morning
- A competitor is mentioned in a deal note → the relevant battle card resurfaces to the rep
- A competitor ships a product launch → battle card auto-updated and the team alerted

See [Triggers](/build/agents/build-your-agent/triggers) and [Workforces](/build/workforces/create-a-workforce).

### L4 — Let it tune itself

The Agent learns which competitor signals actually predicted lost deals and weights monitoring accordingly. It stops alerting on noise nobody acts on, and surfaces only changes that should shift your positioning.

## Common pitfalls

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tracking too many competitors" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Twenty competitors equals alert fatigue and nothing gets read. Limit to the 5–10 that actually surface in deals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Battle cards that read like marketing copy" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Reps can't use a battle card mid-call if it's a webpage. Write them as short objection-handling scripts the rep can read in one breath.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Intel that never reaches a deal" icon="circle-exclamation">
    A weekly digest is interesting. Resurfacing the right battle card the moment a competitor shows up in a deal is useful. Wire the Agent to deal records, not just channels.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sources that are too easy" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Press releases only show you what competitors want you to see. Add hiring patterns, job postings, and customer-side social signals to catch real direction.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
