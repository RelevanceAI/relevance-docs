The Relevance AI Desktop App is a signed macOS application that installs like any other Mac app. This guide walks you through downloading, installing, and authenticating the app.

## System requirements

Before installing, verify your Mac meets these requirements:

- macOS 11.0 (Big Sur) or later
- Active Relevance AI account
- Internet connection for authentication and syncing

## Download the app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the download page">
    Go to [relevanceai.com/desktop](https://relevanceai.com/desktop) or access the download link from your Relevance AI account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the installer">
    Click the download button to get the latest version of the Desktop App for macOS.
    
    The file will be named something like `Relevance-Desktop-1.0.0.dmg`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Install the app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the installer">
    Double-click the downloaded `.dmg` file to mount the disk image.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag to Applications">
    Drag the Relevance AI app icon to your Applications folder.
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/desktop-app/drag-to-applications.png" alt="Drag Relevance AI to Applications folder" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the app">
    Open your Applications folder and double-click the Relevance AI app to launch it.
    
    If you see a security warning about the app being from an unidentified developer, right-click the app and select "Open" to bypass the warning on first launch.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The Desktop App is signed with an Apple Developer certificate, but macOS may still show a security warning on first launch. This is normal for apps downloaded outside the Mac App Store.
</Note>

## Authenticate the app

The Desktop App uses a secure deep-link authentication flow to connect to your Relevance AI account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Sign in'">
    When you first launch the app, you'll see a welcome screen with a "Sign in" button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize in your browser">
    The app will open your default web browser and take you to the Relevance AI authentication page.
    
    If you're not already logged in, enter your Relevance AI credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant authorization">
    Review the permissions the Desktop App is requesting and click "Authorize" to grant access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the app">
    Your browser will redirect you back to the Desktop App using a deep link. The app will automatically complete the authentication process.
    
    <Tip>
    If the redirect doesn't work automatically, copy the authorization code from your browser and paste it into the Desktop App.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Initial setup

After authentication, the app will guide you through the initial setup:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Grant permissions">
    The app will prompt you to grant screen recording and microphone permissions in macOS System Settings.
    
    These permissions are required for the app to detect meetings and record audio.
    
    [Learn more about permissions →](/get-started/desktop-app/permissions)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure preferences">
    Set your recording preferences:
    - Automatic meeting detection (on/off)
    - Audio quality settings
    - Sync preferences
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete setup">
    Click "Get Started" to finish setup and begin using the Desktop App.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verify installation

To confirm the Desktop App is installed and working correctly:

1. Check that the Relevance AI icon appears in your macOS menu bar
2. Click the menu bar icon to open the app interface
3. Verify your account name appears in the app settings
4. Confirm the app status shows "Ready to record"

## Update the app

The Desktop App checks for updates automatically and will notify you when a new version is available.

To update manually:

1. Click the Relevance AI icon in your menu bar
2. Select "Check for updates" from the menu
3. If an update is available, click "Download and install"
4. The app will restart automatically after updating

## Uninstall the app

If you need to remove the Desktop App:

1. Quit the app by clicking the menu bar icon and selecting "Quit"
2. Open your Applications folder
3. Drag the Relevance AI app to the Trash
4. Empty the Trash to complete removal

<Note>
Uninstalling the app does not delete your recordings or transcripts from your Relevance AI account. They remain accessible through the web interface.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting installation

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The app won't open after installation">
    If macOS prevents the app from opening:
    
    1. Right-click the app in your Applications folder
    2. Select "Open" from the context menu
    3. Click "Open" in the security dialog that appears
    
    This bypasses the Gatekeeper security check for apps downloaded outside the Mac App Store.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Authentication fails or times out">
    If the deep-link authentication doesn't work:
    
    1. Make sure your default browser is set correctly in macOS System Settings
    2. Try copying the authorization code from the browser and pasting it into the app
    3. Check that you're logged into the correct Relevance AI account in your browser
    4. Disable any browser extensions that might block redirects
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The app doesn't appear in my menu bar">
    If the menu bar icon is missing:
    
    1. Check that the app is actually running (look in Activity Monitor)
    2. Try quitting and relaunching the app
    3. Check your macOS menu bar settings to ensure the icon isn't hidden
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I can't find the downloaded .dmg file">
    By default, macOS saves downloads to your Downloads folder. If you can't find it:
    
    1. Open Finder and go to Downloads
    2. Sort by "Date Added" to find recent files
    3. Search for "Relevance" in Spotlight (Cmd + Space)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
