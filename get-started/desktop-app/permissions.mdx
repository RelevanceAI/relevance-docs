The Relevance AI Desktop App requires two macOS permissions to function: screen recording and microphone access. This guide explains why these permissions are needed and how to grant them.

## Why permissions are needed

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Screen recording" icon="display">
    Detects when you're in a meeting by monitoring active applications
  </Card>
  <Card title="Microphone" icon="microphone">
    Captures audio from your meetings for transcription
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
The Desktop App only uses these permissions when you're actively recording a meeting. Screen recording is used solely for meeting detection, not to capture your screen content.
</Info>

## Grant screen recording permission

macOS requires explicit permission for apps to access screen recording capabilities.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open System Settings">
    Click the Apple menu in the top-left corner of your screen and select "System Settings" (or "System Preferences" on older macOS versions).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Privacy & Security">
    In the sidebar, click "Privacy & Security."
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/desktop-app/privacy-security.png" alt="Privacy & Security in System Settings" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Screen Recording">
    Scroll down and click "Screen Recording" in the list of privacy options.
    
    You may need to click the lock icon in the bottom-left corner and enter your password to make changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Relevance AI">
    Find "Relevance AI" in the list of apps and toggle the switch to enable screen recording permission.
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/desktop-app/enable-screen-recording.png" alt="Enable screen recording for Relevance AI" />
    </Frame>
    
    <Warning>
    You must quit and restart the Desktop App after granting screen recording permission for the change to take effect.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Grant microphone permission

The Desktop App needs microphone access to capture audio from your meetings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open System Settings">
    Click the Apple menu and select "System Settings" (or "System Preferences").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Privacy & Security">
    In the sidebar, click "Privacy & Security."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Microphone">
    Scroll down and click "Microphone" in the list of privacy options.
    
    You may need to unlock settings by clicking the lock icon and entering your password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Relevance AI">
    Find "Relevance AI" in the list of apps and toggle the switch to enable microphone access.
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/desktop-app/enable-microphone.png" alt="Enable microphone for Relevance AI" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Unlike screen recording, microphone permission changes take effect immediately without requiring an app restart.
</Tip>

## Verify permissions are granted

After granting permissions, verify they're working correctly:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Desktop App">
    Click the Relevance AI icon in your menu bar to open the app interface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check permission status">
    The app will display the status of both permissions in the settings or main interface.
    
    You should see green checkmarks next to both "Screen Recording" and "Microphone."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test meeting detection">
    Open a video conferencing app (Zoom, Google Meet, etc.) to verify the Desktop App detects it.
    
    The app should show a notification or status change indicating a meeting has been detected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting permission issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The app says permissions are denied, but I granted them">
    If permissions appear granted in System Settings but the app still reports them as denied:
    
    1. Quit the Desktop App completely (right-click the menu bar icon and select "Quit")
    2. Reopen System Settings and verify the permissions are still enabled
    3. Restart your Mac if the issue persists
    4. Try removing and re-granting the permissions
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I don't see Relevance AI in the permissions list">
    If the app doesn't appear in the Screen Recording or Microphone lists:
    
    1. Launch the Desktop App at least once to trigger the permission request
    2. Try performing an action that requires the permission (like starting a recording)
    3. Check that the app is installed in your Applications folder, not running from the Downloads folder
    4. Reinstall the app if it still doesn't appear
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Screen recording permission keeps getting revoked">
    If macOS repeatedly revokes screen recording permission:
    
    1. Make sure you're running the latest version of the Desktop App
    2. Check that the app is properly signed (right-click the app, select "Get Info," and verify the signature)
    3. Try removing the app from Screen Recording permissions, restarting your Mac, then re-granting permission
    4. Contact support if the issue continues
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Meeting detection isn't working after granting permissions">
    If the app has permissions but still doesn't detect meetings:
    
    1. Verify both screen recording AND microphone permissions are granted
    2. Make sure automatic meeting detection is enabled in the app settings
    3. Check that your video conferencing app is supported (Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, etc.)
    4. Try manually starting a recording to test if the issue is specific to automatic detection
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I grant permissions without admin access?">
    Granting screen recording and microphone permissions requires administrator access on macOS. If you're using a work computer with restricted permissions:
    
    1. Contact your IT department to request permission changes
    2. Provide them with this documentation to explain what permissions are needed
    3. Some organizations may have policies that prevent granting screen recording access
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Privacy and security

<Info>
The Relevance AI Desktop App takes your privacy seriously:

- Screen recording permission is used only to detect active meeting applications, not to capture screen content
- Microphone access is only active during meetings you choose to record
- All recordings are encrypted during transmission and storage
- You can revoke permissions at any time through macOS System Settings
</Info>

## Revoke permissions

If you need to remove the Desktop App's permissions:

1. Open System Settings > Privacy & Security
2. Select "Screen Recording" or "Microphone"
3. Find "Relevance AI" in the list and toggle the switch off
4. The app will no longer have access to that permission

<Warning>
Revoking permissions will prevent the Desktop App from detecting meetings and recording audio. You'll need to re-grant permissions to use the app again.
</Warning>
